About me

I am currently a JSPS Fellow at Kyushu University in Fukuoka, my host is Professor Takuro Abe.
Previously, I was a Leibniz Fellow at the Mathematisches Forschungsinstitut Oberwolfach, a postdoctoral fellow at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn and a postdoctoral assistant at the Lehrstuhl VI for Algebra and Number Theory of Prof. Dr. Gerhard Röhrle at Ruhr-Universität Bochum. Before that, I was a PhD student at the Insitute for Algebra, Number Theory and Discrete Mathematics at Leibniz Universität Hannover, advised by Prof. Dr. Michael Cuntz.

My research is in the field of algebraic and geometric combinatorics with connections to commutative algebra, algebraic geometry and algebraic topology. I am particularly interested in the interplay between combinatorial, algebraic and topological invariants of discrete geometric objects like hyperplane arrangements, i.e. finite sets of codimension one subspaces in a finite dimensional vector space, and reflection groups.

In particular, my research focuses on:

  • Logarithmic vector fields and free arrangements

    A hyperplane arrangement is called free, if a certain associated graded module over the coordinate ring of the ambient vector space (the module of logarithmic vector fields or derivation module) is free.
    The fundamental open problem is Terao's conjecture from the 1980s asserting that freeness (over a fixed field) only depends on the combinatorics of the arrangement, i.e. its intersection lattice.

  • Sheaves on posets (intersection lattices)

    A finite poset gives rise to a finite topological space whose topology consists of the open sets which are increasing subsets, the so called Alexandroff topology. The poset can also be regarded as a small category. Each functor from the poset to the category of abelian groups or modules over a commutative ring induces a sheaf of abelian groups respectively modules on the Alexandroff space.
    For a hyperplane arrangement there are natural functors on its intersection lattice giving rise to certain sheaves. Their cohomology controls algebro geometric invariants, in particular the freeness of the arrangement.

  • (finite complex) Reflection groups and their associated reflection arrangements

    Finite complex reflection groups are groups generated by linear transformations of finite order with fixed-point space a hyperplane. Naturally associated to each reflection group is its reflection arrangement consisting of all the reflecting hyperplanes.
    These highly symmetric arrangements have always played a prominent role in the study of hyperplane arrangements. They provide interesting examples for many phenomena and in considering this class the deep connections of algebra, combinatorics, geometry and topology in the study of hyperplane arrangements become most clearly apparent.

  • Simplicial arrangements

    A simplicial arrangement is a hyperplane arrangement in a real vector space which divides the ambient space into simplicial cones.
    The class of simplicial arrangements encompasses the class of all real reflection arrangements and their restrictions but contains many more sporadic configurations.
    Despite some partial results, a complete classification especially in higher dimensions is still wide open.

  • Topology of oriented matroids and hyperplane arrangement complements

    In the study of complements of complex hyperplane arrangements it is of particular interest when they are Eilenberg-MacLane K(pi,1)-spaces.
    A central result is Pierre Deligne's theorem stating that the complement of a complexified simplicial arrangement is a K(pi,1)-space. In particular, all reflection arrangements of a finite Coxeter group have this property. In general, it is very hard to identify arrangements with aspherical complements.
    For a complexified real arrangement Mario Salvetti introduced a regular CW-complex modeling the homotopy type of the complement from the combinatorial structure of the associated oriented matroid.
    The study of the combinatorics of oriented matroids and of maps between associated posets provides an interesting new approach to the K(pi,1)-problem for complexes derived from oriented mattroids and therefor complements of complexified real hyperplane arrangements.
